The Fingerprint prompt
I want you to learn how I write emails. Here is exactly what to do. Step 1: Create an empty file called todo.md. Step 2: Pull my last 300 sent emails from Gmail. For each one, add a single line to todo.md with the subject line or a short one-line description. Leave a checkbox in front of each line. Step 3: Work through the list one email at a time. As you read each email, decide which category it belongs in. You pick the categories based on what you actually find in my emails. You will probably end up with 4 to 6. Fingerprint each category as you go. Step 4: Save all your fingerprints in one file called insights.md, with a clearly labeled section for each category. You know what makes a good voice fingerprint better than I do. Capture whatever a literature expert would capture if they were reading my emails and trying to learn to write like me. Check off each item in todo.md as you finish it.
Turn fingerprints into Skills
Read insights.md. For each category inside, create a Claude Skill that teaches Claude to write emails in that exact voice. Name each skill after the category. Save the skills inside the email-buddy folder so they are scoped to this project only.

The Autopilot prompt
Check my Gmail inbox for new emails since your last run. For each one that needs a reply: 1. Read it and decide which category it falls into. 2. Match it to the right Skill in this folder. 3. Use that Skill to draft a reply in my voice. 4. Save the reply as a draft in Gmail. If the email mentions a past meeting, search my Google Drive for the transcript of that meeting and use details from it in the reply.

The Monday Brief
The Monday Brief prompt
Read all my Google Calendar events for the next 7 days. Read all my Gmail threads from the past 14 days where someone is waiting on a reply from me. Write an email to me with the subject line "Your week ahead." The email should have three sections. Section 1: Top 5 priorities for the week, based on the meetings on my calendar and the emails waiting for me. Section 2: 3 things at risk, meaning deadlines I might miss or people who are frustrated. Section 3: 2 people waiting on me who I should reply to first. Save the email as a draft in Gmail.
Make it yours · the interview
Interview me one question at a time, at a cap of 10 questions. Each question should build on my last answer. Goal: Learn what matters most to me when I triage my calendar and my inbox. Explore: · which people should always rise to the top · which projects are priorities right now · which kinds of deadlines matter most · anything else that would change how I rank what to do next After the interview, write me a prompt optimized for Claude Opus 4.6 as of today. Make it information-dense.
